    How to Use the Professional Tax Calculator

    Professional Tax (PT) is a state-level tax deducted from your salary every month. Our calculator covers all 21 states and 1 UT that levy PT in India, with gender-specific slabs (e.g., Maharashtra has different rates for men and women).

    States That Do NOT Levy Professional Tax

    Delhi, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, Haryana, Punjab, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, Goa, and Jammu & Kashmir do not levy any professional tax. If you work in these states, no PT will be deducted from your salary.

    How PT Saves You Income Tax

    Professional Tax paid is fully deductible under Section 16(iii) of the Income Tax Act — available under BOTH old and new tax regimes. For someone in the 30% tax bracket, paying ₹2,500 PT annually saves approximately ₹780 in income tax (₹2,500 × 30% × 1.04 cess). Your effective cost is only ₹1,720 per year.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is Professional Tax the same in all states?

    No. Each state sets its own PT slab rates. The only common rule is the constitutional ceiling of ₹2,500 per year. Maharashtra has gender-specific slabs where women earning up to ₹25,000 are fully exempt.

    Who is responsible for paying Professional Tax?

    For salaried employees, the employer deducts PT from monthly salary and deposits it with the state government. Self-employed professionals must register and pay PT directly via PTEC (Professional Tax Enrolment Certificate).

    Can I claim PT deduction in the new tax regime?

    Yes! Professional Tax is one of the few deductions available under the new tax regime via Section 16(iii). It is deducted automatically from your salary income in Form 16.

    What happens if my employer does not deduct PT?

    The employer faces penalties and interest for non-compliance. The liability is on the employer, not the employee. However, you should verify PT deduction on your salary slip and Form 16.

    Do remote workers pay PT based on employer location or home location?

    PT is based on your work location, not your employer's registered location. If you work from home in Maharashtra for a Delhi-based company, Maharashtra PT applies.
