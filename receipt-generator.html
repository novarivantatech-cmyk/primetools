What is a Payment Receipt?

A payment receipt is a formal document issued by a seller or service provider to a buyer or client, acknowledging that a payment has been received. It serves as proof of payment and is essential for accounting, tax filing, and dispute resolution. In India, receipts are crucial for GST compliance, HRA claims, and income tax deductions under Section 80G for donations.

Types of Receipts

Payment Receipt

Issued when a customer pays for goods or services. Includes details of items purchased, amount paid, payment mode, and transaction reference. Essential for both B2B and B2C transactions in India.

Rent Receipt

Issued by a landlord to a tenant acknowledging rent payment. Required for salaried employees to claim House Rent Allowance (HRA) exemption under Section 10(13A). Must include landlord's PAN if annual rent exceeds Rs. 1,00,000.

Donation Receipt

Issued by registered charitable organizations to donors. Required to claim tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. Must include the organization's 80G registration number and the donor's PAN.

Legal Requirements for Receipts in India

GST-Compliant Receipts

For transactions above Rs. 200, a GST-registered business must issue a receipt or tax invoice containing: seller's GSTIN, buyer's details (if registered), HSN/SAC codes, taxable value, and GST breakup (CGST/SGST or IGST).

Rent Receipts for HRA

To claim HRA exemption, employees must submit rent receipts to their employer. If annual rent exceeds Rs. 1,00,000, the landlord's PAN is mandatory. The receipt should clearly state the rental period, property address, and amount.

Donation Receipts for 80G

Only donations to organizations with valid 80G registration are eligible for tax deduction. The receipt must contain: donor's name and PAN, donation amount, date, purpose, and the organization's 80G registration number and validity period.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a receipt legally binding?

Yes, a receipt serves as legal proof of payment. In case of disputes, it can be used as evidence in court. However, for large transactions, it is advisable to also have a formal contract or invoice.

What is the difference between a receipt and an invoice?

An invoice is a request for payment issued by the seller before payment is made. A receipt is proof of payment issued after the payment is received. The invoice says "you owe this"; the receipt says "you paid this."

Do I need GST on a receipt?

If you are a GST-registered business and the transaction value exceeds Rs. 200, you should issue a GST-compliant receipt (or tax invoice) showing CGST and SGST (intra-state) or IGST (inter-state).

Can I claim HRA without rent receipts?

No. Rent receipts are mandatory to claim HRA exemption. If your employer does not require them, you must still maintain them for your records. The Income Tax Department may ask for them during scrutiny.

What is the 80G deduction limit for donations?

For donations to eligible charitable institutions, you can claim 50% or 100% deduction depending on the organization, subject to 10% of adjusted gross total income. Always verify the organization's 80G registration status on the Income Tax portal before donating.
